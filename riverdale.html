When the term "milkshake" was first used in print in 1885, milkshakes were an alcoholic whiskey drink that has been described as a "sturdy, healthful eggnog type of drink, with eggs, whiskey, etc., served as a tonic as well as a treat".[4] However, by 1900, the term referred to "wholesome drinks made with chocolate, strawberry, or vanilla syrups."
